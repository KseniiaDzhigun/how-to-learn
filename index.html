Main Problems in Education

Neither in school nor in college are we taught how to properly study materials. We prepare for exams and memorize questions. We practice solving repetitive problems to perform better on tests, but often this doesn't give us real knowledge. Memorization quickly fades away and doesn't bring any benefits.

Conclusion: we also need to learn how to learn, but for some reason, not many places teach that. What should we do about it?

Specific techniques and exercises help change the approach to learning, making it effective and engaging. These same techniques are applied in the case of education at Yandex Praсtiсum.

Learning Techniques

Five Practices from Barbara Oakley

  • Two Types of Attention

    Deep knowledge arises by alternating between focused and diffuse thinking.

  • Recall

    Recall what you've learned — it helps connect scattered pieces of memory.

  • Interleaving

    Study multiple skills simultaneously, they enrich each other.

  • Questions

    While listening to a teacher, come up with a good question that truly interests you.

  • Illusion of Competence

    Repeat to yourself, write down, explain to a friend: you only think you master a new topic.

For those who love to procrastinate

Barbara Oakley's Story.

Since childhood, Barbara struggled with mathematics. She considered herself a complete humanitarian, and not particularly intelligent at that. In the military, she studied the Russian language to earn extra pay, and she excelled to the point of being promoted to a commander. However, to advance in her career, she needed to pass mathematics exams. That's when Barbara came up with her own approach to exact sciences. It turned out that if something is "difficult" for you, the knowledge you gain through hard work is much deeper than those who understand everything at first glance.
